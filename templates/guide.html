CookieGuide
Some YouTube videos require you to be signed in to watch them — age-restricted content, members-only videos, or private unlisted links. VORTEX can use your browser's login cookies to authenticate with YouTube on your behalf. This guide walks you through exporting and uploading them in under two minutes.
Steps
1
Install the cookie exporter extension

You need a browser extension that can export cookies in Netscape format — the format yt-dlp understands. The recommended one is Get cookies.txt LOCALLY.

⬡ Chrome Web Store 🦊 Firefox Add-ons
✓  This extension works locally and does not send your cookies anywhere — it only writes a file to your computer.
2
Sign in to YouTube

Open youtube.com in the same browser where you installed the extension and make sure you are signed in to your Google account.

🌐
Navigate to youtube.com
👤
Click Sign In in the top-right corner if you are not already signed in
✅
Confirm your profile picture appears — you are now signed in
3
Export your cookies

While on youtube.com, click the extension icon in your browser toolbar.

🧩
Click the Extensions puzzle-piece icon in your toolbar, then click Get cookies.txt LOCALLY
📋
A popup appears. Make sure the domain shows youtube.com
💾
Click Export — your browser will download a file called youtube.com_cookies.txt
Get cookies.txt LOCALLY
Domain:  youtube.com
Cookies found:  34
Export as cookies.txt
⚠  Never share your cookies.txt file with anyone. It acts like a login session token and gives full access to your account.
4
Upload cookies to VORTEX

Go back to the VORTEX home page. You will see a 🍪 Browser Cookies panel near the top of the page.

🖱
Drag your cookies.txt file onto the dashed drop zone — or click it to open a file picker
🟢
The status bar turns green and shows the file size when cookies are active
▶
Paste your YouTube URL and download as normal — authentication is now automatic
✓  Your cookie file is stored only on this server and is never sent anywhere else. Click Clear in the panel to remove it at any time.
5
When cookies expire

YouTube session cookies typically last a few weeks to a few months. If you start getting sign-in errors again, just repeat steps 2–4 to export a fresh cookie file and re-upload it.

🔄
Click Clear in the cookie panel on the home page to remove the old file
💾
Export a new cookies.txt from your browser while signed in to YouTube
📂
Drop the new file onto the VORTEX cookie panel

Ready to Download

Cookies uploaded — go back and grab that age-restricted video.
